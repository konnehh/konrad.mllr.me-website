Why Linux?

I started my career as an administrator of mixed Windows and Linux infrastructure while working my apprenticeship in Stuttgart. Originally I had planned to learn mostly about the Windows world and build computers and help people use them. While my Linux journey started out as simple attempts to host a websites and services for me and my friends to use, I quickly learned to enjoy a simple fact: Linux talks. It may speak a complicated language, but it talks. It was hard for me to get Linux to work at first. As it turns out, that was not the fault of Linux: It did exactly what I told it to do, and often I gave it incomplete or wrong information. Using Linux is an exercise in understanding what you tell your system, and trying to listen to what it tells you. I urge you to try it!
